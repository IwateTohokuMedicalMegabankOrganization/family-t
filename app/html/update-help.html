1. Enter the family member’s personal information. Gender is required. A name is required for yourself and optional for others, but names will help you keep track, especially for larger families.
  2. Chose an option for “Living?” If living, enter a date of birth, or an age or estimated age, in years. If not living, enter an age or age range at death, and a cause of death. NOTE: A date of birth is required for a living family member if you will later convert your data file to share with them for recording their family data.
  3. Enter other information such as twin status, adopted status, and height and weight, when requested.
  4. Enter diseases and conditions for this person:
    1. If none, skip this section
    2. Select a disease or condition from the dropdown list titled "Disease or Condition"
    3. If the phrase "(more options…)" appears next to the disease category you choose, also make a selection from the Specific Subtype dropdown list that appears
    4. Select the age of onset (age when the disease was first noticed) from the offered ranges in the dropdown list of that name
    5. IMPORTANT: Press the “Add” button to link the entry to the family member’s data
    6. A new data entry row will then appear to allow you to enter another disease or condition; if not needed, leave it blank and go on to the next section
    7. A “Remove” button will also appear in case you want to delete items already added
  5. Enter other, optional information, where asked
    1. Indicate if your parents are related to each other in any way other than being married
    2. Select each race and ethic background that applies. You can select more than one of each
